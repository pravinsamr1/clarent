SCRM Strategy & Implementation

Cybersecurity SCRM Strategy & Implementation Plan (C-SCRM)

Securing the Supply Chain. Strengthening Digital Trust.

Modern organizations operate within complex, globally distributed technology supply chains. Every vendor, system component, software dependency, and service provider introduces potential cyber risk.

Clarent360’s Cybersecurity Supply Chain Risk Management (C-SCRM) Strategy & Implementation Plan enables organizations to identify, assess, and manage cybersecurity risks across the entire supply chain lifecycle — ensuring operational resilience, regulatory alignment, and trusted digital operations.

Clarent360’s C-SCRM program provides user-oriented operational guidance designed to integrate cybersecurity supply chain risk management into enterprise risk governance.

The program establishes a Concept of Operations (CONOPS) framework that aligns:

  • Organizational mission and operational objectives

  • Enterprise risk management practices

  • Cybersecurity governance expectations

  • Technology and vendor ecosystem oversight

Through a structured implementation approach, Clarent360 transforms supply chain security from a compliance activity into a strategic business capability.

Concept of Operations (CONOPS)

The C-SCRM Strategy & Implementation Plan translates broad cybersecurity supply chain principles into actionable risk management activities. Clarent360 applies a multi-level, C-SCRM-specific methodology to:

Threat Identification

Identify threats impacting suppliers, partners, and technology ecosystems

Safeguard Evaluation

Evaluate technical and non-technical safeguards

Control Assessment

Assess effectiveness of cybersecurity and privacy controls

Governance Integration

Integrate supply chain risk into enterprise governance structures

Continuous Oversight

Enable continuous oversight and executive visibility

This approach ensures cybersecurity risks associated with products and services are visible, measurable, and actively managed across the organization.

Framework Objectives

  • Develop enterprise-wide guidelines for cybersecurity supply chain risk management
  • Protect the Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability, and Safety (CIAS) of systems, applications, services, and data
  • Address risks across hardware, software, and service supply chains
  • Govern cybersecurity and privacy risks within highly networked environments
  • Maintain proactive controls through continuous development, review, and improvement

Capability Need

Global supply chains are increasingly targeted by threat actors seeking to compromise the integrity, trustworthiness, and authenticity of technology products and services. Clarent360’s C-SCRM Strategy & Implementation Plan is designed to:

  • Identify, assess, and mitigate risks arising from distributed technology supply chains
  • Provide structured oversight to executive leadership
  • Enable Line of Business (LOB) stakeholders to understand and manage supply chain risks
  • Strengthen accountability across vendors, partners, and internal teams
  • Deliver expert advisory and education on cybersecurity supply chain practices

Purpose of Cybersecurity SCRM

Cybersecurity Supply Chain Risk Management (C-SCRM) represents the organized and intentional management of cyber risk throughout the supply chain lifecycle, operating at the intersection of:

  • Security: Ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability of supply chain operational information, intellectual property, and supplier data.

  • Suitability: Verifying supplied products and services are appropriate, trustworthy, and aligned with organizational objectives.

  • Safety: Ensuring products do not introduce conditions causing operational disruption, equipment damage, or personal risk.

  • Reliability: Ensuring systems and services perform predictably and securely throughout their lifespan.
