Our Zero Trust delivery model covers identity, device, application, and network layers — mapping implementations to NIST SP 800-207, Microsoft Zero Trust principles, and your organisation's specific risk and compliance requirements.

INTERACTIVE FRAMEWORK

Pillars of a Robust Zero Trust Implementation

Zero Trust is a journey, not a single deployment. Each pillar addresses a core component of the Zero Trust architecture — working together to establish continuous verification, minimal access, and assumed-breach posture across the enterprise.

Verify Identity

Enforce strong authentication — MFA, passwordless, FIDO2 — with adaptive policies that respond to identity risk signals continuously.

Validate Devices

Ensure every device accessing resources meets defined compliance baselines — managed, patched, and enrolled in endpoint management platforms.

Control Access

Apply least-privilege access controls at the application and data layer — scoping permissions to role, context, and session requirements.

Segment Networks

Implement micro-segmentation to isolate workloads, contain lateral movement, and prevent compromised hosts from reaching critical systems.

Inspect & Monitor

Maintain continuous visibility into access patterns, session activity, and anomalous behaviour across all users, devices, and applications.

Respond & Adapt

Automate response to detected anomalies — revoking sessions, blocking devices, and escalating incidents without waiting for human intervention.
