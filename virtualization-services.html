Pillars of a Robust Virtualisation Programme

A secure virtualisation environment requires more than deploying a hypervisor. Each pillar addresses a distinct layer of the virtualisation stack — ensuring the platform is performant, resilient, and protected from the hypervisor upward.

Plan & Size

Assess workload requirements, storage needs, and network topology before platform selection — ensuring the virtualisation design fits operational and budget constraints.

Deploy & Configure

Install and configure the hypervisor platform, storage backend, networking, and management tooling to documented, security-aligned standards.

Harden & Secure

Apply CIS Benchmark hardening to hypervisors, management interfaces, and virtualised network segments — locking down the attack surface at the platform level.

Integrate & Connect

Connect virtualisation platforms to enterprise identity, monitoring, backup, and ticketing systems — enabling unified operations and audit visibility.

Protect & Recover

Implement backup, snapshot, and disaster recovery capabilities — ensuring virtualised workloads can be restored within defined RTO and RPO targets.

Monitor & Maintain

Establish ongoing performance monitoring, capacity management, and patch lifecycle processes to keep the platform secure and operationally stable.
