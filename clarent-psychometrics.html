Anchor Intelligence Framework

Mental health & wellbeing screening for young people — under 18

Under 18 Clinical oversight required 12 assessments
CADENCE · ANCHOR
All Anchor assessments require delivery by, or under direct supervision of, a qualified school counsellor, psychologist or child welfare practitioner. Assessments ANC-05 and ANC-07 carry mandatory immediate escalation protocols and must only be administered where a clinician can respond in real time.

A trauma-informed, clinically aligned wellbeing and risk screening framework for young people under 18. Designed for school counsellors, educational psychologists, child welfare services, and youth health organisations. Anchor is a screening tool, not a diagnostic instrument — it flags for qualified professionals to act on, not conclude from.

Domain A — Wellbeing & Distress Screening · ANC-01 to ANC-04
ANC-01

Psychological Wellbeing Index

Baseline emotional and psychological health screening using an age-appropriate youth variant of validated wellbeing instruments.

ANC-02

Anxiety & Worry Pattern Assessment

Generalised anxiety indicators, worry frequency, physical symptom correlation and avoidance behaviour screening.

ANC-03

Low Mood & Depressive Indicator

Early-stage depressive symptom detection — persistent low mood, anhedonia and withdrawal — with structured clinician follow-up protocol.

ANC-04

Stress & Coping Style Profile

How young people currently manage pressure — adaptive vs. maladaptive coping strategy mapping across academic, social and family stressors.

Domain B — Risk & Safety Screening · ANC-05 to ANC-08 Mandatory escalation
ANC-05

Self-Harm Risk Indicator

Non-clinical early-warning screen for non-suicidal self-injury with mandatory immediate clinical escalation and safeguarding notification.

ANC-06

Trauma Exposure & Impact Screening

Adverse childhood experience indicators and psychological impact assessment identifying trauma affecting current functioning.

ANC-07

Suicidal Ideation Flag Protocol

Safe messaging-compliant early detection with mandatory immediate practitioner escalation, parent notification and crisis service referral.

ANC-08

Substance Risk Behaviour Screen

Alcohol and substance experimentation indicators linked to emotional regulation deficits, trauma responses and peer pressure vulnerability.

Domain C — Social & Relational Health · ANC-09 to ANC-12
ANC-09

Peer Connection & Loneliness Index

Social isolation indicators and quality of peer and family relational bonds — predictors of mental health vulnerability and school engagement.

ANC-10

Cyberbullying & Online Safety Screen

Digital harm exposure, social media distress patterns and online identity pressure impact on mental health and sleep.

ANC-11

Family & Home Environment Profile

Protective factor and risk factor assessment for the home context — parental support, household stress, stability and attachment quality.

ANC-12

Resilience & Protective Strength Map

Identify existing internal and external resilience assets to anchor intervention — strengths-based, never deficit-led.
