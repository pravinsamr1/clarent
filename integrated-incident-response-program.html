Integrated Incident Response Program

Coordinated Response. Resilient Operations. Confident Recovery.

Cyber incidents are no longer isolated technical events — they are enterprise business risks requiring coordinated, organization-wide response.

The Clarent360 Integrated Incident Response Program (IIRP) establishes a unified framework that enables organizations to detect, respond to, and recover from cybersecurity and privacy incidents through structured governance, trained personnel, and integrated operational practices.

Program Overview

The Integrated Incident Response Program provides user-focused operational guidance designed to align people, processes, and technology into a single coordinated incident response capability.

Clarent360’s approach adopts an integrated systems perspective, ensuring that incident response activities support organizational mission objectives, operational priorities, and executive expectations.

The program enables organizations to implement secure, repeatable incident response processes that promote proactive risk management across the enterprise.

Concept of Operations (CONOPS)

The Clarent360 IIRP defines how incident response operates within an organization from a strategic and operational viewpoint.

The program ensures:

  • Consistent implementation of cybersecurity and privacy incident response practices

  • Integration of technical and non-technical safeguards

  • Enterprise visibility into incident management activities

  • Alignment between operational teams and executive leadership

Incident response success depends on more than technology. The program ensures:

  • Appropriate technologies are deployed

  • Personnel are trained and prepared
