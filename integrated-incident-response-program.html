Integrated Incident Response Program

Coordinated Response. Resilient Operations. Confident Recovery.

Cyber incidents are no longer isolated technical events — they are enterprise business risks requiring coordinated, organization-wide response.

The Clarent360 Integrated Incident Response Program (IIRP) establishes a unified framework that enables organizations to detect, respond to, and recover from cybersecurity and privacy incidents through structured governance, trained personnel, and integrated operational practices.

Program Overview

The Integrated Incident Response Program provides user-focused operational guidance designed to align people, processes, and technology into a single coordinated incident response capability.

Clarent360’s approach adopts an integrated systems perspective, ensuring that incident response activities support organizational mission objectives, operational priorities, and executive expectations.

The program enables organizations to implement secure, repeatable incident response processes that promote proactive risk management across the enterprise.

Concept of Operations (CONOPS)

The Clarent360 IIRP defines how incident response operates within an organization from a strategic and operational viewpoint.

The program ensures:

  • Consistent implementation of cybersecurity and privacy incident response practices

  • Integration of technical and non-technical safeguards

  • Enterprise visibility into incident management activities

  • Alignment between operational teams and executive leadership

Incident response success depends on more than technology. The program ensures:

  • Appropriate technologies are deployed

  • Personnel are trained and prepared

Capability Need

Modern organizations operate within highly interconnected digital ecosystems where incidents impact multiple business functions simultaneously.

The Clarent360 Integrated Incident Response Program exists to ensure that incident response responsibilities are:

  • Visible to Line-of-Business stakeholders

  • Clearly understood by asset and process owners

  • Governed through executive oversight

  • Supported by expert advisory and education capabilities

The program provides leadership with the ability to hold stakeholders accountable for maintaining effective incident response management practices.

Operating Concept

Clarent360 aligns incident response practices with globally recognized cybersecurity frameworks such as:

ISO 27002
NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
NIST SP 800-53 / 800-171
CIS Critical Security Controls

Organizations adopting the IIRP work toward a defined maturity objective of establishing standardized, secure, and repeatable incident response practices across the enterprise.

Building a Practiced Response Capability

An effective incident response program requires organizational readiness — not just documentation.

Clarent360 helps organizations develop a practiced capability where:

All users are trained to recognize suspicious activity

Reporting processes are clear and accessible

Dedicated incident responders execute coordinated actions

Response activities follow an established Incident Response Plan (IRP)

Key Outcomes

Organizations implementing the Clarent360 Integrated Incident Response Program achieve:

Faster detection and containment of incidents

Accelerate threat identification and isolate affected resources swiftly to minimize operational disruption.

Coordinated enterprise response capability

Unify cross-functional security and operational teams under a single incident response framework.

Improved executive visibility and governance

Enable transparent dashboards and reports to keep senior leadership aligned during security events.

Reduced operational disruption

Minimize downtime by utilizing practicing guidelines and robust Business Continuity workflows.

Strengthened privacy and data protection practices

Embed compliance requirements directly into technical procedures to secure personal and business data.

Increased organizational cyber resilience

Develop a practiced, continuously-improving capability built to adapt to the modern threat landscape.
